## Legal Review Checklist

Provide this checklist to your attorney for review:

### 1. Jurisdiction & Governing Law

- [ ] **Governing law clause** specifies correct state/country
- [ ] **Jurisdiction clause** specifies appropriate courts
- [ ] **Choice of law** is enforceable in both parties' jurisdictions
- [ ] **Venue** is appropriate for your business location

**Questions for Attorney**:
- What jurisdiction should govern this agreement?
- Do we need separate terms for EU partners (GDPR compliance)?
- Should we include arbitration clause instead of court venue?

---

### 2. Intellectual Property

- [ ] **IP ownership** clearly states all RegEngine IP remains with Provider
- [ ] **Feedback license** is appropriate (perpetual, royalty-free)
- [ ] **Partner IP** protection is adequate (if Partner provides proprietary data)
- [ ] **Derivative works** clause covers improvements made during evaluation

**Questions for Attorney**:
- Is our feedback license too broad or too narrow?
- What if Partner creates derivative works during evaluation?
- How do we handle joint inventions?

---

### 3. Liability & Warranties

- [ ] **Disclaimer of warranties** ("AS IS") is enforceable in our jurisdiction
- [ ] **Limitation of liability** cap is reasonable
- [ ] **Exclusion of consequential damages** is enforceable
- [ ] **Indemnification** (if any) is mutual and fair

**Questions for Attorney**:
- Is our liability cap too low to be enforceable?
- Should we include indemnification clause?
- Do we need professional liability insurance?

---

### 4. Confidentiality

- [ ] **Confidential Information definition** is clear and comprehensive
- [ ] **Exclusions** from confidential information are standard
- [ ] **Obligations** of Receiving Party are enforceable
- [ ] **Term of confidentiality** extends beyond agreement termination

**Questions for Attorney**:
- Should confidentiality survive termination? For how long?
- Do we need mutual NDA or one-way (we disclose more)?
- What about residual knowledge clause?

---

### 5. Data Protection & Privacy

- [ ] **Data restrictions** prohibit ingestion of PII/sensitive data
- [ ] **GDPR compliance** (if EU partners) is addressed
- [ ] **Data deletion** obligations upon termination are clear
- [ ] **Security incident notification** timeline is appropriate

**Questions for Attorney**:
- Do we need separate Data Processing Agreement (DPA)?
- What are our obligations under GDPR/CCPA if Partner uploads personal data?
- Should we require Partner to sign data use restrictions?

---

### 6. Regulatory Compliance

- [ ] **Regulatory disclaimer** states Platform does not provide legal advice
- [ ] **No reliance** clause prevents Partner from using output for compliance decisions
- [ ] **Validation requirement** makes clear Partner must verify all outputs
- [ ] **Sanctions compliance** (if applicable) addresses export controls

**Questions for Attorney**:
- Is our regulatory disclaimer strong enough?
- Do we need explicit "not a law firm" language?
- What about unauthorized practice of law concerns?

---

### 7. Termination

- [ ] **Termination for convenience** notice period is reasonable
- [ ] **Termination for cause** cure period is appropriate
- [ ] **Effect of termination** clearly states data deletion requirements
- [ ] **Survival clauses** identify which provisions continue post-termination

**Questions for Attorney**:
- What provisions should survive termination?
- Should we allow immediate termination for material breach?
- What about transition assistance obligations?

---

### 8. Miscellaneous Provisions

- [ ] **Entire agreement** clause is present
- [ ] **Amendment** requirements are clear (written, signed by both parties)
- [ ] **Severability** clause protects if one provision is unenforceable
- [ ] **Waiver** clause prevents oral modifications

**Questions for Attorney**:
- Do we need force majeure clause?
- Should we include assignment restrictions?
- What about counterpart execution (electronic signatures)?

## Industry-Specific Considerations

### If Partner is in Financial Services

- [ ] Review with fintech/banking attorney
- [ ] Consider FINRA/SEC compliance implications
- [ ] Address third-party service provider requirements
- [ ] Review SOC 2 / ISO 27001 audit implications

### If Partner is in Healthcare

- [ ] Review HIPAA compliance (no PHI in sandbox)
- [ ] Consider BAA (Business Associate Agreement) if PHI possible
- [ ] Address FDA regulated data restrictions
- [ ] Review HITECH Act notification requirements

### If Partner is in EU

- [ ] GDPR compliance review mandatory
- [ ] Consider Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
- [ ] Address data transfer mechanisms
- [ ] Review EU representative requirements
