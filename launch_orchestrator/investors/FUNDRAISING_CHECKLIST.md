- [ ] **One-Pager** (1 page)
  - Status: TODO - Condense pitch deck to 1 page
  - Use case: Quick email attachment, in-person handout

- [x] **Data Room Structure**
  - File: `DATA_ROOM_STRUCTURE.md`
  - Status: Template complete, need to populate actual documents
  - Platform: Google Drive or DocSend

### Investor Research

- [ ] **Build Target List** (50-100 investors)
  - Sources: Crunchbase, AngelList, VC firm websites
  - Filter by: Thesis (RegTech/FinTech/B2B SaaS), Stage (Seed), Geography, Check size
  - Export to spreadsheet: `investor_tracking.xlsx`

- [ ] **Identify Warm Intro Paths**
  - For each investor: Check LinkedIn for mutual connections
  - Prioritize: 2nd-degree connections with strong ties
  - Document in spreadsheet: Connection name, relationship strength

- [ ] **Research Each Firm**
  - Portfolio companies (especially competitors)
  - Recent investments (thesis evolution)
  - Partner bios (who leads fintech/RegTech deals?)
  - Fund status (currently deploying? or between funds?)

### Tracking & Tools

- [ ] **Set Up CRM** (HubSpot, Streak, or Airtable)
  - Stages: Target ‚Üí Outreach ‚Üí First Call ‚Üí Deck Sent ‚Üí Partner Meeting ‚Üí Diligence ‚Üí Term Sheet
  - Fields: Investor name, firm, email, stage, last contact date, next step

- [ ] **Set Up DocSend** (or Google Drive)
  - Upload pitch deck, investor memo
  - Enable analytics tracking
  - Set expiration dates (90 days)

- [ ] **Set Up Calendly** (or similar)
  - Create "Investor Meeting" event type (45 min)
  - Buffer time before/after meetings
  - Limit availability (don't appear too desperate)

### Team Alignment

- [ ] **Founder Alignment**
  - Agree on valuation range ($6M-$10M pre-money typical for seed)
  - Agree on dilution target (15-25% typical)
  - Agree on "must-have" vs. "nice-to-have" in term sheet
  - Decide who handles investor relations (primary contact)

- [ ] **Legal Prep**
  - Engage startup attorney (if not already)
  - Prepare standard documents (cap table, incorporation cert, etc.)
  - Discuss SAFE vs. priced round

- [ ] **Reference Prep**
  - Identify 3-5 design partners willing to take reference calls
  - Brief them on what investors will ask
  - Coordinate availability

## Week 1-2: Initial Outreach

### Warm Intros (Priority 1)

- [ ] **Day 1-3: Request Warm Intros**
  - Send "forwardable blurb" to 10-20 mutual connections
  - Template: `INVESTOR_OUTREACH_SEQUENCE.md` ‚Üí Forwardable Blurb
  - Goal: 10 warm intros secured

- [ ] **Day 4-7: Follow Up on Intros**
  - Thank the connector
  - Send deck to interested investors
  - Schedule first calls

### Cold Outreach (Priority 2)

- [ ] **Day 1-7: Send Cold Emails**
  - Send 20 cold emails (personalized)
  - Template: `INVESTOR_OUTREACH_SEQUENCE.md` ‚Üí Cold Email Template
  - Timing: Tuesday-Thursday, 9am-11am local time
  - Goal: 5-10 responses (10-20% response rate)

### Conference/Event Outreach (Priority 3)

- [ ] **Identify Relevant Events**
  - Fintech conferences: Money20/20, Fintech Meetup, LendIt
  - Demo days: Y Combinator, Techstars (if applicable)
  - VC office hours: Check firm websites for open office hours

- [ ] **Attend & Network**
  - Bring one-pagers to hand out
  - Collect business cards
  - Follow up within 24 hours

## Week 3-4: First Meetings

### Meeting Preparation

- [ ] **Rehearse Pitch**
  - Practice with co-founder, advisors
  - Time yourself (10-minute pitch, 45-minute meeting)
  - Prepare for common objections (see `INVESTOR_FAQ.md`)

- [ ] **Prepare Demo**
  - Live API walkthrough (have backup recording in case WiFi fails)
  - Show: Ingestion ‚Üí Extraction ‚Üí Graph Query ‚Üí Arbitrage Detection
  - Duration: 5-7 minutes max

- [ ] **Prepare Q&A**
  - Review `INVESTOR_FAQ.md`
  - Have financial model ready to share screen
  - Have customer testimonials ready to reference

### Conduct Meetings (Target: 15-20 first meetings)

- [ ] **First Meeting Script**
  - 5 min: Intros, small talk
  - 10 min: Problem + Solution story
  - 10 min: Product demo (live)
  - 10 min: Market + business model
  - 10 min: Q&A

- [ ] **Post-Meeting Follow-Up** (within 24 hours)
  - Send thank-you email
  - Send deck + memo if not already sent
  - Send requested materials (financial model, tech docs, etc.)
  - Schedule next step (partner meeting or follow-up call)

### Tracking

- [ ] **Update CRM After Each Meeting**
  - Log: Meeting date, attendees, key questions, investor sentiment
  - Set next action: Send deck, schedule partner meeting, etc.
  - Flag: Hot (very interested), Warm (interested), Cold (pass)

## Week 5-6: Partner Meetings & Second Calls

### Partner Meeting Preparation

- [ ] **Deeper Materials**
  - Prepare detailed financial model walkthrough
  - Prepare technical architecture deep dive
  - Prepare competitive analysis (detailed)

- [ ] **Conduct Partner Meetings** (Target: 5-10 partner meetings)
  - Structure: 15 min recap, 15 min product deep dive, 15 min financials, 15 min Q&A
  - Attendees: Multiple partners (sometimes 3-5 people)
  - Dress code: Business casual (match firm culture)

### Reference Calls

- [ ] **Coordinate Reference Calls**
  - Connect investors with design partners
  - Brief design partners on what to emphasize
  - Template: `INVESTOR_OUTREACH_SEQUENCE.md` ‚Üí Reference Call Coordination

- [ ] **Debrief After Reference Calls**
  - Ask design partner: What did investor ask? Any concerns?
  - Follow up with investor: Address any concerns raised

## Week 7: Diligence & Term Sheet Negotiation

### Due Diligence Prep

- [ ] **Provide Data Room Access**
  - Share link to data room (Google Drive / DocSend)
  - Organize by category (see `DATA_ROOM_STRUCTURE.md`)
  - Track which investors view which documents

- [ ] **Respond to Diligence Requests**
  - Financial model deep dives
  - Technical architecture questions
  - Legal doc reviews (incorporation, IP assignment, etc.)
  - Customer references

### Term Sheet Negotiation

- [ ] **Review Term Sheet**
  - Valuation: $6M-$10M pre-money (target)
  - Dilution: 15-25% (target)
  - Board seat: Typically 1 investor seat for seed
  - Pro-rata rights: Standard (allows investor to maintain ownership % in future rounds)
  - Liquidation preference: 1x non-participating (standard for seed)
  - Anti-dilution: Broad-based weighted average (standard)

- [ ] **Consult Attorney**
  - Review all terms with startup attorney
  - Identify any unusual/unfavorable terms
  - Negotiate problematic terms

- [ ] **Negotiate**
  - Valuation: If lowball, counteroffer with justification (ARR traction, comparable deals)
  - Terms: Push back on: super pro-rata, participating preferred, full-ratchet anti-dilution
  - Timing: Don't accept first offer immediately (signals desperation), but don't drag out (signals indecision)

### Create Urgency (if needed)

- [ ] **Share Term Sheet News with Other Investors**
  - Template: `INVESTOR_OUTREACH_SEQUENCE.md` ‚Üí Term Sheet Received
  - Message: "We've received a term sheet, $300k remaining, decision needed by [date]"
  - Goal: Convert warm investors to committed investors

## Week 8: Closing

### Final Diligence

- [ ] **Legal Diligence**
  - Provide: Certificate of Incorporation, Bylaws, Cap Table, IP assignments
  - Answer any questions from investor's counsel

- [ ] **Reference Checks**
  - Investor may call founders, advisors, customers
  - Prep everyone on key talking points

- [ ] **Background Checks** (if applicable)
  - Some firms do background checks on founders
  - Be upfront about anything that might surface

### Documentation

- [ ] **Execute Term Sheet**
  - Sign non-binding term sheet
  - Announce to other investors (create FOMO for remaining allocation)

- [ ] **Prepare Closing Documents**
  - Stock Purchase Agreement (SPA) or SAFE agreement
  - Investor Rights Agreement (IRA)
  - Right of First Refusal (ROFR) agreement
  - Amended cap table

- [ ] **Sign Closing Documents**
  - Review all documents with attorney
  - Sign via DocuSign or similar
  - Countersign from investors

### Wire Transfer & Closing

- [ ] **Receive Funds**
  - Confirm wire instructions with investors
  - Verify funds received in company bank account
  - Send confirmation to investors

- [ ] **Announce**
  - Press release (optional for seed, common for Series A+)
  - LinkedIn post (founder + investors tag each other)
  - Update website (add investors to "Backed by" section)
  - Email list update (if you have one)

- [ ] **Welcome Email**
  - Thank investors
  - Introduce to team
  - Set expectations for updates (monthly investor updates)

- [ ] **Set Up Investor Updates**

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### Fundraising Process

1. ‚ùå **Starting too late**: Begin fundraising 3-6 months before you *need* the money (not when you're running out)
2. ‚ùå **Too few investors**: Need to talk to 50-100 investors to close 1-3. Don't stop at 10.
3. ‚ùå **Long gaps between meetings**: Momentum is critical. Schedule meetings densely (multiple per week).
4. ‚ùå **No urgency**: Create urgency with closing deadlines. Don't let fundraise drag on 6+ months.
5. ‚ùå **Taking first term sheet**: Always get 2-3 term sheets to negotiate best terms.

### Pitch Mistakes

1. ‚ùå **Too much product, not enough market**: Investors invest in *markets*, not features. Spend 50% of pitch on market/problem.
2. ‚ùå **Weak financials**: Have a bottoms-up financial model. Don't just say "we'll get 1% of a $10B market."
3. ‚ùå **Dismissing competition**: Never say "we have no competitors." Show you understand competitive landscape.
4. ‚ùå **No customer proof**: Need customer testimonials, usage data, or signed LOIs. Investors don't bet on PowerPoints.

### Term Sheet Mistakes

1. ‚ùå **Optimizing only for valuation**: Terms matter as much as valuation. A $10M valuation with bad terms < $8M with good terms.
2. ‚ùå **Taking money from wrong investor**: Investor fit matters. Check references (talk to their portfolio companies).
3. ‚ùå **Not reading fine print**: Super pro-rata, participating preferred, full-ratchet anti-dilution are red flags.
